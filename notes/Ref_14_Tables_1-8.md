## Notes

E_lepton denotes the lepton beam energy of the beam used in the experiment (measured at 27.6 GeV).  
The lepton type(s) used in this experiment were electrons and positrons.

The hadron used in the experiment was a proton, with a rest-energy constant of 0.938 GeV.

Both satisfy the (e^±)p → (e^±)pγ condition.  

Collaboration was done with the HERMES Spectrometer.

Data are taken from Figures and Tables 2 and 3 (page 7) of the paper.

Events are selected with exactly one photon producing an energy deposition larger than 5 GeV (1 MeV) in the calorimeter (preshower detector) and one charged track, identified as the scattered lepton

Some Kinematic requirments were imposed:  
- 1.0 < Q² < 10 (GeV)²   
- 0.03 < x_b < 0.35
- W^2 > 9 GeV^2
- v < 22 GeV

The angle between the laboratory three momenta q and q' was limited to be less than 45 mrad

(θ_{γ*γ} < 45 mrad) 

- a missing-mass-squared cut –1.5 < M_X² < 1.7 (GeV/c²)²

This region was shifted by 0.18 GeV2 for the exclusive events from e−(electron) data.

## Relation of Tables to Excel Sheets

There are 8 Excel Sheets total, based off of the different bins and functions present in Tables 2 & 3. 

Each Excel Sheet is seperated based off of each respective Fourier coefficients/amplitudes. The definitions for these values are found in the table below.

 **Table Column names and their corresponding Excel Sheet names**

average -t, x_B, or Q^2 values = t, xB, and Q2

Type of Fourier function = obs

Amplitude of (corresponding Fourier function) = value

±δ(stat.) ±δ(syst.) are indicated as syst_u & stat_u 

## Definitions & Units Table

| Symbol          | Meaning                                                                                                                                     | Units                |
|:---------------:|:--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|:---------------------|
| `E_beam`        | Incident lepton (electron or positron) beam energy                                                                                          | 27.6 GeV             |
| `M_p`           | Proton rest mass                                                                                                                             | 0.938 GeV/\(c^2\)     |
| `Q^2`           | Photon virtuality, \(Q^2 \equiv -\,q^2 = -(k - k')^2\)                                                                                        | (GeV/\(c\))²         |
| `x_B`           | Bjorken scaling variable, \(x_B = Q^2/(2\,p\cdot q)\)                                                                                         | —                    |
| `ξ`             | Skewness, fractional longitudinal momentum transfer to the parton (\(\xi \approx x_B/(2 - x_B)\) in the generalized Bjorken limit)           | —                    |
| `t`             | Mandelstam variable, four-momentum transfer squared to the proton, \(t = (p - p')^2\)                                                         | (GeV/\(c\))²         |
| `φ`             | Azimuthal angle between the lepton scattering plane and the real-photon production plane                                                      | degrees (°)          |
| `φ_S`           | Azimuthal angle of the target’s transverse spin vector \(\mathbf{S}_\perp\) relative to the lepton scattering plane                          | degrees (°)          |
| `S_⊥`           | Magnitude of the target polarization component perpendicular to the virtual-photon direction                                                 | —                    |
| `λ`             | Beam helicity (\(+1\) or \(-1\))                                                                                                             | —                    |
| `e`             | Beam charge (\(+1\) for \(e^+\), \(-1\) for \(e^-\))                                                                                         | —                    |
| `y`             | Fractional energy transfer, \(y = (p\cdot q)/(p\cdot k)\)                                                                                     | —                    |
| `ε`             | Virtual-photon polarization parameter, \(\varepsilon = 2\,x_B\,M_p/\sqrt{Q^2}\)                                                               | —                    |
| `ν`             | Virtual-photon energy in the target rest frame, \(\nu = (p\cdot q)/M_p\)                                                                      | GeV                  |
| `W`             | Invariant mass of the photon–nucleon system, \(W^2 = M_p^2 + 2\,M_p\nu - Q^2\)                                                                | GeV/\(c^2\)          |
| `M_X^2`         | Missing-mass squared of the undetected final state \(X\), \(M_X^2 = (q + p - q')^2\)                                                           | (GeV/\(c^2\))²       |
| `P_1(φ),P_2(φ)` | Lepton-propagator functions entering the Bethe–Heitler denominator                                                                           | —                    |
| `K_BH`          | Bethe–Heitler kinematic factor, \(K_{BH} = [x_B^2\,t\,(1+\varepsilon^2)]^{-1}\)                                                              | —                    |
| `K_{DVCS}`      | DVCS kinematic factor, \(K_{DVCS} = 1/Q^2\)                                                                                                   | —                    |
| `K_I`           | Interference kinematic factor, \(K_I = 1/(x_B\,y\,t)\)                                                                                        | —                    |
| `c_{n,*}, s_{n,*}` | Fourier coefficients (cos \(nφ\) or sin \(nφ\)) for the BH, DVCS or interference terms (see Eqs. 3–5)                                       | —                    |
| `A_C^(cos nφ)`  | \(n\)th–harmonic coefficient of the **beam-charge asymmetry** (cos \(nφ\) moment)                                                           | —                    |
| `A_{LU,I}^{sin nφ}`  | \(n\)th–harmonic **sin \(nφ\)** moment of the **beam-spin asymmetry** from the BH–DVCS **interference** term                            | —                    |
| `A_{LU,DVCS}^{sin φ}` | Leading **sin φ** moment of the **pure DVCS** contribution to the beam-spin asymmetry                                                   | —                    |
| `A_{UT,*}`      | Various target-spin asymmetry amplitudes (sin (φ − φ_S), cos (φ − φ_S) sin nφ, etc.) for BH, DVCS and interference (see Eqs. 24–27)            | —                    |
